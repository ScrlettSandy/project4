BudgetBunny

BudgetBunny is a student-focused budgeting app concept I created to make managing money feel less intimidating and more visually engaging. I designed the app around the idea that budgeting should feel approachable, motivating, and easy to return to, especially for students balancing spending, savings, and everyday financial decisions.

Project Overview

A budgeting app concept made for student life.

For this project, I created BudgetBunny as a mobile app concept focused on helping students track spending, build saving habits, and feel more in control of their finances. I wanted the experience to feel polished and welcoming rather than stressful, so I combined clear information hierarchy with playful branding, simple navigation, and reward-based features. The result is an app concept that feels practical, visual, and encouraging to use.

What I Did

I developed the concept, planned the structure, created wireframes in Figma, and designed the final screens. I thought through how the app would look, how a student user would move through it, and how to make the interface feel consistent across different pages.

Tools + Focus

  • Figma for wireframing and interface design
  • UX thinking and screen hierarchy
  • Student-centered app planning
  • Visual branding and UI consistency
  • Designing a more approachable budgeting experience
My Process

From concept to final screens.

I worked through this project in stages, starting with the problem I wanted to solve and then building out the structure, visuals, and app flow. I used Figma to create wireframes first, then moved into styling and refining the interface so it felt cohesive and user-friendly.

1

Idea

I began with the idea of making a budgeting app for students that would feel supportive, clear, and visually engaging instead of overwhelming.

2

Wireframes

I created wireframes in Figma to map out the app’s main screens, including onboarding, dashboard, goals, and account features.

3

UI Design

I designed the final interface with rounded cards, clear icons, consistent layouts, and a branded visual system that makes the app feel polished and memorable.

4

Refinement

I refined spacing, hierarchy, colour consistency, and navigation so the screens worked together as one complete and cohesive app concept.

Features

What BudgetBunny includes.

Spending Dashboard

A main dashboard shows users their monthly total in a bold, easy-to-understand way so they can quickly see their current spending.

Weekly Goals

Progress tracking helps make budgeting feel more manageable by breaking it into smaller, more motivating goals.

Rewards + Streaks

Reward icons and streak systems encourage consistent habits and add a more interactive, positive feeling to the experience.

Student Resources

The app includes sections for things like OSAP, scholarships, discounts, flights, and other helpful student finance-related resources.

Profile Area

A profile page gives users access to account information, badges, and status details in the same visual style as the rest of the app.

Friendly UX

I wanted the app to feel approachable, clean, and visually soft so that money management would feel less stressful for the user.

Reflection

What I learned from this project.

BudgetBunny helped me think more carefully about how visual design shapes the emotional experience of an app. Since budgeting can often feel stressful, I wanted to create an interface that felt calm, supportive, and rewarding instead. Through designing the app in Figma, I learned more about how importnt consistency is across screens, especially with layout, hierarchy, colour, and icon use.


This project also helped me develop my skills in wireframing and thinking through user flow. I had to consider both function and feeling: how the app should work, but also how it should make the user feel. Overall, BudgetBunny reflects my interest in UX, branding, and creating digital experiences that are polished, memorable, and user-centered.
