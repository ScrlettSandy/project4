BudgetBunny

BudgetBunny is a student-focused budgeting app concept I created to make money management feel less overwhelming and more approachable. The app was designed to help students keep track of spending, build saving habits, view rewards, and access helpful financial tools in a way that feels simple, friendly, and motivating.

Explore Project View Screens
Project Overview

An app concept designed for student budgeting.

For this project, I designed BudgetBunny as a mobile app concept that helps students stay aware of their spending while also making financial goals feel more visual and rewarding. I wanted the experience to feel inviting rather than stressful, so I focused on soft colours, rounded shapes, simple navigation, and playful reward-based features. The goal was to create something practical, but also encouraging and easy to return to regularly.

What I Did

I developed the app concept, planned the screen flow, created wireframes, explored the visual direction, and built the interface in Figma. I also thought through the user journey, layout hierarchy, icon use, and how to make the design feel cohesive across multiple screens.

Tools + Focus

  • Figma for wireframing and UI design
  • User-focused app planning
  • Screen hierarchy and navigation
  • Branding through colour, icons, and tone
  • Designing a more engaging budgeting experience
My Process

From idea to interface.

I approached this project step by step, starting with the idea of creating a budgeting app that would feel less intimidating for students. From there, I worked through structure, screen planning, visual identity, and final interface refinement.

1

Concept Development

I started by defining the app’s purpose: helping students manage spending, savings, and rewards in one friendly place.

2

Wireframing

I created wireframes in Figma to map out the structure of the app, including the welcome screen, home page, goals section, and account page.

3

Visual Design

I built out the final screens using a mint-green palette, soft shadows, rounded cards, and playful bunny-themed visuals to make the design feel calm and approachable.

4

Refinement

I refined spacing, hierarchy, consistency, and icon usage so that the screens felt cohesive and easy to navigate as a complete app system.

Key Features

What the app includes.

Spending Tracking

A home screen focused on showing current monthly spending in a bold, easy-to-read way so users can quickly understand where they stand.

Weekly Goals

Progress bars help turn budgeting into smaller goals, which makes saving and spending habits feel more manageable and motivating.

Rewards + Streaks

Visual rewards and streak systems make the experience feel more interactive and encouraging, especially for student users who benefit from positive reinforcement.

Student Resources

The interface includes quick access to practical student-focused areas such as OSAP, scholarships, discounts, flights, and information.

Personal Profile

A profile section lets users view account details, achievements, and status while keeping the design consistent with the rest of the app.

Friendly Visual Identity

The bunny imagery, soft palette, and rounded UI were designed to make a finance app feel less harsh and more welcoming.

Reflection

What I learned from designing BudgetBunny.

This project helped me think more deeply about how visual design can change the emotional experience of an app. Since budgeting can feel stressful, I wanted to design something that felt calm, motivating, and easy to use. Through creating the wireframes in Figma and developing the final interface, I learned how important consistency is across screens, especially with spacing, icons, colour, and hierarchy.


I also learned how much small design choices matter when building trust and usability. Features like progress tracking, rewards, and simplified navigation made the concept feel more engaging and student-friendly. Overall, BudgetBunny reflects my interest in UX, app design, and creating digital experiences that feel polished, supportive, and visually memorable.
